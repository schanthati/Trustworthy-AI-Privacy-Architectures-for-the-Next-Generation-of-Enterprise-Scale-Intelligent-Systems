### Overview

This reference architecture illustrates a trust-by-design artificial intelligence framework for enterprise-scale intelligent systems operating within regulated and governance-sensitive environments.

### Key Architectural Components

#### Signals

Captures operational, transactional, telemetry, customer interaction, and risk-related data sources used by enterprise artificial intelligence systems.

#### Ingestion and Privacy

Implements secure data ingestion, schema governance, encryption controls, tokenization mechanisms, and personally identifiable information (PII) isolation procedures.

#### Trust Controls

Applies governance orchestration, role-based access controls (RBAC), attribute-based access controls (ABAC), policy enforcement mechanisms, and jurisdiction-aware operational controls.

#### Intelligence Layer

Provides Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG), controlled knowledge retrieval, explainability mechanisms, model inference services, and safety controls.

#### Enterprise Action

Supports workflow execution, business process management, case management operations, human-in-the-loop review processes, and automated enterprise actions.

### Foundational Controls

#### Audit and Observability

Supports immutable event logging, end-to-end traceability, data lineage documentation, model lineage tracking, and audit evidence generation.

#### Model Lifecycle and Risk Management

Incorporates version control, approval workflows, continuous monitoring, change management procedures, rollback mechanisms, and operational governance controls.

* Policy enforcement before inference
* Explainability by construction
* End-to-end auditability
* Governance-aware orchestration

## Enterprise Trustworthy AI – Technical Architecture

![Enterprise Trustworthy AI Technical Architecture](architecture.png)

This architecture presents a trust-by-design artificial intelligence framework integrating privacy-preserving data pipelines, governance-aware orchestration, controlled knowledge retrieval, Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG), intelligent decisioning, enterprise workflow execution, and continuous operational oversight.

The architecture begins with enterprise data sources, including transactional systems, operational logs, sensors, application interfaces, and business applications. Information is processed through privacy-preserving data pipelines that apply validation, security, encryption, tokenization, and data quality controls before entering governance-aware orchestration layers.

Governance controls enforce policy requirements, access management controls, workflow orchestration, and decision-routing mechanisms throughout the artificial intelligence lifecycle. The intelligence layer incorporates knowledge retrieval, semantic search, vector-based retrieval, and Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) capabilities to support context-aware artificial intelligence operations.

Artificial intelligence inference and decisioning components provide model-driven recommendations, decision support, explainability functions, confidence evaluation, and human-review mechanisms. Enterprise action and integration services support workflow automation, application integration, notifications, and operational execution across enterprise environments.

The architecture is supported by foundational controls including auditability, traceability, model lifecycle management, risk management, accountability controls, governance oversight, security controls, privacy safeguards, compliance mechanisms, resilience capabilities, backup processes, recovery functions, monitoring systems, and observability frameworks.

This framework demonstrates system-level architectural approaches for developing resilient, accountable, audit-ready, governance-aware, and trustworthy artificial intelligence environments suitable for enterprise and regulated technology ecosystems.

## Real-Time Trustworthy AI Architecture

![Real-Time Trustworthy AI Architecture](architecture.png)

This diagram presents an enterprise-grade artificial intelligence architecture designed around privacy-preserving, governance-aware, audit-ready, and workflow-integrated system principles.

The architecture shows how event and data sources move through secure ingestion, privacy controls, governance orchestration, inference and retrieval, and enterprise workflow execution.
